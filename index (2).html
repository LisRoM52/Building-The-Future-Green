Introduction

LEED certification evaluates buildings based on sustainability, energy efficiency, water efficiency, material use, and indoor environmental quality.

LEED Certification

LEED certification provides a framework for healthy, highly efficient, and cost-saving green buildings.

What Are Green Buildings?

Green buildings are structures designed to reduce environmental impact and improve occupants' well-being.

Notable LEED-Certified Buildings in the Philippines

  • Proscenium by Rockwell Workspaces (Makati City): Operable windows, VRF inverter air conditioning system, and more.
  • The Galleon by Ortigas Land (Pasig): Solar reflectivity study, good ventilation, and energy efficiency.
  • Filinvest City (Muntinlupa): Gold certification for Neighborhood Development with green spaces and zero-carbon public transport.
  • Sevina Park (Laguna): Rainwater collector, renewable energy, and endemic trees.

Government Policies and Support

  • Philippine Green Building Code: Promotes sustainability in construction.
  • Local Government Incentives: Tax reductions and expedited permit processes for green buildings.
  • RA 11285: Mandates energy efficiency measures and renewable energy integration.
